## Audit Section: Third-Party Citation Gap

**What it measures:** Whether AI engines have reasons to trust your brand beyond your own website. AI answers weight independent mentions — reviews, industry press, directories, expert commentary — more heavily than anything you publish yourself.

**What we check (6 items):**

1. **Citation inventory** — Where your brand is currently mentioned: industry publications, news, directories, review platforms (Google Business Profile, ProductReview.com.au, industry-specific), podcasts, and expert roundups.
2. **Competitor citation gap** — The 3 competitors most cited by AI engines for your target queries, and which sources cite them but not you.
3. **Source authority mapping** — Which of your existing citations AI engines actually draw from (we test your target prompts across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Claude and trace the cited sources).
4. **Review signal health** — Volume, recency, and rating consistency across platforms AI engines pull from.
5. **Entity corroboration** — Whether third-party sources confirm the same facts your website claims (name, location, services, credentials). Mismatches erode AI confidence.
6. **Wikipedia/Wikidata presence** — Eligibility check, not a promise. Most SMBs don't qualify; adjacent entity listings often do.

**Client deliverable:** A ranked citation-gap list — the 10 specific sources most likely to move your AI visibility, ordered by (impact × achievability), each with the concrete action required.

**Positioning line for the sales page:**

> "Your website tells AI who you are. Third parties tell AI whether to believe you. We audit both."

**Notes:**

- Item 3 is the differentiator — no Australian generalist agency is tracing actual AI citations back to source.
- The deliverable being a ranked action list (not a report) justifies the $1,500–$2,500 price point.
