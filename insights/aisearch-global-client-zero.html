The Starting Point

AISearch Global launched in May 2026. The site was built from day one with AEO intent — Organization and ProfessionalService schema on the homepage, a 51-question FAQPage, clear entity signals, a dedicated insights section, an AEO Score Calculator. The whole thing was designed to model best practice.

Intent is not execution.

When we ran a systematic audit in June 2026 — the same process we use for client work — we found ten specific issues that were suppressing AI visibility. None of them were catastrophic. All of them were fixable. Most of them were the same issues we find on client sites.

That last point matters: even a site built with AEO expertise ships with gaps. A single developer working across an entire site will miss things. An audit finds them.

What the Audit Found

The findings broke across all four layers of the AEO Traction Stack. Layer 1 (Entity Clarity) was the strongest area. Layers 2, 3 and 4 all had meaningful gaps.

01
No FAQPage schema on six service pages
Layer 2 — Schema Markup

The homepage had a 51-question FAQPage schema. The six individual service pages — AEO Audit, Content Restructuring, Schema Implementation, AI Brand Presence, Structured Content Systems, AI Visibility Strategy — had none. From an AI perspective, each service page was an unstructured block of prose. No machine-readable Q&A. No structured answers for AI to extract and cite.

Why it matters: Service pages are high-intent. They are the pages AI is most likely to cite when recommending a specific service. Unstructured service pages lose citation opportunities to competitors with structured ones.

Fixed — FAQPage schema added to all 6 service pages
02
FAQPage schema buried inside Article schema
Layer 2 — Schema Markup

The What Is AEO insight article had FAQPage schema — but it was nested inside the Article schema as a mainEntity property. Some AI crawlers handle this correctly. Others don't. Best practice is a standalone FAQPage block at the top level, separate from the Article schema.

Fixed — FAQPage schema separated into its own block
03
No Speakable schema anywhere on the site
Layer 2 — Schema Markup

Speakable schema tells AI assistants (and voice search) which sections of a page are most suitable for reading aloud or extracting as a spoken answer. It is a direct citation signal for AI overviews. The entire site launched without it — none of the insight articles, none of the FAQ page, none of the service pages.

Fixed — Speakable schema added to FAQ page and all insight articles
04
No HowTo schema on the framework article
Layer 2 — Schema Markup

The AEO Traction Stack article describes a four-layer implementation process — exactly the content HowTo schema exists to structure. AI systems use HowTo schema to extract step-by-step processes for featured answers and AI overviews. The article shipped without it.

Fixed — HowTo schema added with all four implementation steps
05
Near-zero internal links across insight articles
Layer 3 — Answer-Format Content

The three published insight articles had almost no links between them or to service pages. Internal linking does two things for AI visibility: it helps AI crawlers understand the relationship between your content, and it routes citation authority through the site. Without it, each article exists in isolation — a cluster of one.

Fixed — internal links added to all three insight articles
06
Meta descriptions over the recommended length
Layer 1 — Entity Clarity

Nine pages had meta descriptions that exceeded recommended length. The homepage was the worst at 291 characters — almost double the ~155-character guideline. While meta descriptions are not a direct AI ranking signal, truncated descriptions in search results reduce click-through, and excessively long descriptions can indicate a lack of conciseness that carries through to content structure.

Fixed — meta descriptions trimmed on 9 pages
07
Title tags missing geo qualifiers on key pages
Layer 1 — Entity Clarity

Four pages — including the core AEO Audit service page — had title tags without geographic qualifiers. For a location-based service targeting Australian businesses, geo signals in title tags strengthen local entity clarity. "AI Visibility Audit" is a generic phrase; "AI Visibility Audit Australia" is a locally anchored entity signal.

Fixed — title tags updated on 4 pages including geo qualifiers
08
Insights index page had no ItemList schema
Layer 2 — Schema Markup

The insights hub at /insights/ lists published articles but had no ItemList or CollectionPage schema to tell AI systems this is a structured content index. AI crawlers benefit from explicit structured data that identifies content collections — it aids content discovery and strengthens topical authority signals.

Fixed — ItemList schema added to insights/index.html
09
Sitemap contained invalid entries
Layer 4 — Citation Consistency

The sitemap.xml included /#contact (an anchor, not a page) and /llms.txt (a flat text file, not an HTML page). Both are invalid sitemap entries. A sitemap with invalid entries signals poor site hygiene to crawlers and can reduce confidence in the validity of the remaining entries.

Fixed — both entries removed, sitemap resubmitted
10
dateModified not updated on published articles
Layer 3 — Answer-Format Content

Article schema includes a dateModified field. All three published insight articles still showed their original publication date even after content had been updated post-launch. AI systems use recency signals when deciding which sources to cite — outdated modification dates can suppress citations even when the content is current.

Fixed — dateModified updated across all three articles

What This Tells You

The core lesson

A site built with AEO awareness still shipped with ten fixable issues. Not because the builder didn't know better — but because building and auditing are different cognitive tasks. You cannot audit your own work with the same eye you use to build it.

The same is true for your business. Your web developer or marketing team may know what good looks like. That does not mean every page of your site reflects it. Schema gets missed on service pages. Internal links get skipped when you're in a hurry to launch. Speakable markup is easy to forget when it's not core to the page template.

The gap between "we know AEO" and "our site passes an AEO audit" is where your AI visibility is being lost.

The ten issues above took two weeks to find and one day to fix. The score moved from 52 to 74 — from Grade D to Grade B. The remaining gap (from 74 to a higher score) is in content depth: more insight articles, visible FAQ sections on service pages, deeper citation building across external directories.

Those are the Layer 3 and Layer 4 improvements from the AEO Traction Stack — and they take longer. But the Layer 2 fixes — the schema work — delivered the majority of the improvement in a single day of implementation.

This is consistent with what the Traction Stack predicts: 80% of AI visibility improvement comes from Entity Clarity and Schema Markup. The audit found the gaps. The fixes proved the framework.
