What do businesses ask about AEO?

The questions Australian businesses ask most about AI visibility and getting found in AI answers.

What is answer engine optimisation (AEO)?

AEO is the practice of making your business visible, citable, and recommendable in AI-generated answers — ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, DeepSeek, and Grok. Traditional SEO targets keyword rankings. AEO targets the AI response your customer reads before they ever click a link. Four things drive it: entity clarity, schema markup, answer-format content, and citation consistency.

How is AEO different from SEO?

SEO gets you ranked in a list of links. AEO gets you cited inside the answer. A business can rank on page one of Google and not appear anywhere in AI-generated answers — because AI systems use different signals. AEO and SEO are complementary, but they require different strategies. Most businesses doing SEO have done nothing for AEO yet.

How do I know if AI is currently recommending my business?

Ask directly. Open ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google and type what your customers would type — "best [your service] in [your suburb]". If your business isn't named, you have an AEO gap. The free AEO Score Calculator checks 20 signals (17 structural AEO + 3 Princeton GEO) in under a minute — no email required.

Is AEO a one-time fix or ongoing work?

Both. The foundation — entity clarity, schema markup, and content restructuring — is a one-time setup taking 4–8 weeks, and it accounts for roughly 80% of measurable improvement. After that, most businesses run a light check every 6–12 months as AI platforms update their citation logic. You don't need a retainer to see results.
